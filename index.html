Our Story

Why these two products. Why agencies. Why now.

Here's the honest framework: if your product's value is "put a prompt into ChatGPT for you and show the output in a nicer UI," you're dead. Every AI product that's just a wrapper will die as models get cheaper and more accessible. That's what killed our first product.

But there are two things raw AI models fundamentally cannot do, and this is where your surviving products have real ground to stand on:

Models can't connect to your data and run persistently. ChatGPT can't replicate that because ChatGPT doesn't have access to your agency's data, doesn't run on a schedule, and doesn't maintain state across weeks. ClientPulse isn't an AI wrapper — it's a data aggregation product with AI intelligence on top. The integrations, the persistent scoring, the weekly automated brief — that's the product.

Models can't run a multi-step pipeline end-to-end. With ReForge, the value isn't "AI writes a LinkedIn post." The value is: upload one podcast, content intelligence extracts 15 segments, generates 50+ outputs in your voice across 16 platforms — you review in Kanban, approve, publish directly to X/LinkedIn/WordPress, track performance, learn what works. That's a workflow, not a prompt.

"We're not selling AI. We're selling a system that uses AI. The AI is the engine. The product is the car."

Every product must have a data moat.

ReForge's Voice Engine compounds brand voice data with every piece you approve. ClientPulse's Health Score trains on your specific client patterns. Six months in, no competitor can replicate what you've built — because the intelligence is uniquely yours.

One connector to first value.

ReForge needs one transcript. ClientPulse needs one Stripe OAuth click. No 4-step onboarding. No "connect your CRM + calendar + Gmail + Zoom minimum." If the intelligence is good, this alone drives retention.

Products must compound over time.

Recursive learning means every interaction — every approved draft, every client save, every Monday Brief — feeds back into the system. The voice engine is noticeably better at month 3 than day 1. That's the retention hook.
